What's going on?

For the 2026 season, MLB implemented the ABS (Automated Ball-Strike) challenge system. The strike zone is now calculated precisely based on each batter's measured height: 27% to 53.5% of their height.

This meant players had to be officially measured, shoes off, back against the wall. For the first time, we have actual heights instead of the self-reported numbers that teams have used for decades.

Turns out a lot of players were... optimistic about their heights.
